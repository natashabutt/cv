Projects

Mitigating Gender Bias in Occupation Classification

For my MSc natural language processing (NLP) group project, we chose to investigate the effectiveness of graph convolutional networks (GCNs) for the occupation classification of job biographies then to measure and mitigate the associated gender bias in the predictions. This was achieved by implementing in Python the Text GCN of Yao et al. (2018), Graph Convolutional Networks for Text Classification, derived from Kipf and Welling (2017), Semi-Supervised Classification with Graph Convolutional Networks. Our dataset consisted of ~100k biographies, a subset set of that used for De-Arteaga et al (2019), Bias in Bios: A Case Study of Semantic Representation Bias in a High-Stakes Setting. An article that I wrote on this project may be found here. We were pleased to received 91% from the UCL NLP department and plan to extend this project to look at bias mitigation through adversarial training on a range of tasks and models.

Bayesian Modelling of Stop and Search in the UK

For my MSc summer thesis, my aim was to investigate whether there is evidence of disproportionate use of stop and search in the UK. This was achieved through fitting Poisson generalised linear mixed models with Bayesian inference using Gibbs sampling in R with BUGS. My data sources included ~1.9m stops and searches and ~11.5m crimes from the UK Police, and socio-economic statistics on ~1.5m individuals from the 2011 UK Census. Firstly, I modelled the rate of stop and search in proportion to arrests, allowing for police force heterogeneity. Next, I looked at the rate of stop and search in proportion to resident populations at a more granular geographical level and so introduced an intrinsic conditional autoregressive effect to enable spatial smoothing. An article I wrote on the non-spatial modelling of stop and search in proportion to arrest may be found here.
